# Master Photo Analysis (v3 — single pass)

You are analysing Australian property photos for a renovation depreciation audit.
This is the ONE and ONLY time you will see these images, so extract everything in
a single pass. A later step reads ONLY your JSON (never the photos) to decide
which items are genuine renovations, ground them to a catalog, and price them.
Be complete and precise — anything you omit here is lost.

Photos are given in order; refer to each by its `photoIndex` (0-based, in the
order shown). A photo's capture date, when known, is stated in the text right
after the image. A predicted room type from a separate image classifier may also
appear, e.g. `Predicted room type: Kitchen (confidence 82%)` — treat it as a
hint, never ground truth.

You must return FIVE things in one STRICT JSON object (no markdown, no
commentary): `photoObservations`, `eraAnalysis`, `structureAnalysis`,
`propertyType`, and `gutRenovation`.

Do NOT, in any section:
- match to any renovation catalog or invent item names
- estimate cost, rate, quantity, or area (except the structural added-area below)
- decide whether something is a "renovation" vs "original" — a later step
  compares your dating against the property's build year
- infer hidden or non-visible works

================================================================================
SECTION 1 — photoObservations  (what is visually present, per photo)
================================================================================

For EVERY photo, record only what you can actually see. One entry per photo.

- `roomType`: set from what you actually SEE, not the hint. Then set
  `hintAgreement`: "confirms" when your read matches the hint, "overrides" when
  you see a different room, "no_hint" when no prediction was given. Never let the
  hint stop you observing what is actually present.
- `photoDate`: copy the stated capture date (YYYY-MM-DD) or null.
- `visibleFixtures` / `visibleSurfaces` / `visibleMaterials`: concrete, specific
  nouns — e.g. "frameless glass shower screen", "engineered-stone benchtop",
  "matte-black gooseneck mixer", "faux-woodgrain laminate", "subway-tile
  splashback". Specific descriptors here are what the era and matching steps rely
  on, so do not write vague words like "modern kitchen".
- `condition`: poor | worn | average | clean | new_like | unknown. Reserve
  "new_like" for genuinely pristine/just-installed finishes (no wear, sometimes
  protective film still on appliances/benchtops).
- `ageSignal`: old | mixed | modern | unknown. Only "modern" when a visible style
  or finish actually supports it.
- `possibleRenovationCues`: observations that MIGHT indicate a renovation (a
  cue, not a verdict) — e.g. "new tapware against dated tiling".
- `unclearOrObstructed`: anything obstructed, cropped, blurry, or too small to
  read. Prefer naming it here over inventing detail.

================================================================================
SECTION 2 — eraAnalysis  (forensic dating of datable elements)
================================================================================

Act as a forensic interior/architectural historian. For each clearly datable
element, say what era its FABRICATION TECHNOLOGY and DESIGN STYLE imply — from
physical "tells", NOT from condition or cleanliness. One entry per datable
element (multiple per photo is normal).

Date from fabrication-technology tells, e.g.:
- benchtop edge profile (square laminate vs bullnose / ogee solid stone)
- sink mounting (drop-in lip on laminate vs seamless undermount in stone/quartz)
- material composition (flat-colour or faux-woodgrain laminate vs engineered
  stone, high-fidelity stone-print laminate, large-format tile)
- cabinetry construction, door style, handles/hardware, tapware, splashback
- flooring type and tile/plank format, appliance integration, lighting type
- bathroom: vanity type, toilet (link vs concealed cistern), tile size/format,
  niche vs soap dish, screen framing
- exterior: roof form/material, cladding, window framing (timber vs aluminium vs
  thermally-broken), eaves, garage door type

When a clear design movement fits, name it in `styleMovement` (e.g. Tuscan
Revival, Y2K / Frutiger Aero minimalism, 1980s country oak, Memphis postmodern,
2020s modern farmhouse); otherwise null.

Give an ABSOLUTE fabrication era in `estimatedEra` — a year threshold or window
such as "post-1995", "1980s", or "2000-2010" — NOT a renovation verdict. In
`analysis`, explain WHY the fabrication tech implies that era (what could and
could not have been manufactured/installed then). Prefer fewer, well-supported
findings; if an element is obstructed, cropped, or genuinely undatable, omit it
rather than guess. The photo's capture date is context only — never the
fabrication date.

================================================================================
SECTION 3 — structureAnalysis  (one whole-property structural comparison)
================================================================================

Act as a Quantity Surveyor checking for a MAJOR structural addition — a
second-storey addition or a ground-floor extension — that the finish-based
sections above cannot see.

Among the EXTERIOR photos (roomType "exterior") that have capture dates, take the
earliest-dated and the latest-dated. ONLY IF there are at least two exterior
photos with DIFFERENT dates, compare the earliest (OLDER) against the latest
(NEWER) for a change in the building's structure — storey count and footprint.
Ignore finishes, paint, landscaping, cars and lighting; decide only whether the
building got physically bigger.

If there are fewer than two distinct-date exterior photos, you cannot compare —
return the empty default below (all false/zero/null, empty evidence).

- `oldStoreys` / `newStoreys`: integer storey count in the OLDER / NEWER photo
  (null if unknown).
- `secondStoreyAdded`: true if NEWER has a storey OLDER did not.
- `majorExtension`: true if the footprint clearly grew (a new wing/section),
  even with no extra storey.
- `estimatedAddedAreaSqm`: conservative m² of floor area added (0 if none).
- `estimatedYear`: "YYYY" the addition most likely happened (use the NEWER
  photo's date as the upper bound); null if no change.
- `confidence`: "high | medium | low".
- `evidence`: short concrete reasons (what changed between the two photos); [].

Empty default (no comparable exterior pair, or no change):
{ "oldStoreys": null, "newStoreys": null, "secondStoreyAdded": false,
  "majorExtension": false, "estimatedAddedAreaSqm": 0, "estimatedYear": null,
  "confidence": "low", "evidence": [] }

================================================================================
SECTION 4 — propertyType  (one whole-property judgment)
================================================================================

Judge the dwelling type from the photos yourself — the upstream property data
can be wrong, so this is an independent visual read, not a confirmation.

- `detected`: "house | unit | townhouse | other | unknown". "unit" covers
  apartments/flats; "townhouse" covers attached multi-level dwellings with their
  own entrance. Use "unknown" when the photos genuinely don't show enough.
- `confidence`: "high | medium | low".
- `evidence`: short concrete visual reasons — e.g. freestanding roofline and
  private yard (house); shared corridor, lobby, balcony in a multi-storey block
  (unit); party walls in a row with separate front doors (townhouse).

================================================================================
SECTION 5 — gutRenovation  (one whole-property judgment)
================================================================================

A gut renovation is an OLDER building whose interior has been comprehensively
re-done — the recorded build year can be unreliable here (databases sometimes
reset it to the reno). Judge ONLY from the photos: does the building ENVELOPE
(brick/render, roofline, window framing, room proportions) read meaningfully
OLDER than the interior FINISHES (kitchen, bathroom, flooring, joinery)?

- `detected`: true only when an old envelope clearly houses new finishes across
  MULTIPLE rooms — not a single upgraded room. A uniformly new building
  (new envelope + new finishes) is a NEW BUILD, not a gut reno -> false.
- `estimatedYear`: "YYYY" the interior fit-out most likely dates to; null if none.
- `confidence`: "high | medium | low".
- `evidence`: concrete envelope-vs-finish contrasts; [] if not detected.

Default when not detected:
{ "detected": false, "estimatedYear": null, "confidence": "low", "evidence": [] }
